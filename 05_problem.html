Ismail ibn Musa Menk

Ismail ibn Musa Menk (born 27 June 1975), commonly known as Mufti Menk, is a Zimbabwean Islamic speaker. He is the Grand Mufti[5][6] of Zimbabwe's Muslim community,[7][8] and head of the fatwa department for the Council of Islamic Scholars of Zimbabwe.

Early life

Menk was born on 27 June 1975 in Salisbury, Rhodesia to an Indian Muslim family of Gujarati ancestry.[9] He undertook his initial studies with his father, memorizing the Quran and learning Arabic.[10] He went to St. John's College (Harare) for senior school.[11] He studied Jurisprudence and Shariah in Madinah.[12] He specialised post grad in the Hanafi School of thought in Darul Uloom Kantharia in Gujarat, India. Menk has been identified as a Deobandi[11][13][14] as well as a Salafi.[15]
